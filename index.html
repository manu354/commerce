Buying a phone

Think of these 4 key things beforehand and you cant go wrong!
These are the four main stages you should go through when getting a phone:

Do I need a phone?

Do you need a new phone just because you have a cracked screen? Or just because its a year old? Do you even need a phone if you have an ipad?

Budget

How much money do you have? Is it worth paying an extra $300 dollars for a better phone? Remember you don't need to get the most expensive phone!

Research

Sped some time researching the phone you want to buy! Don't get the iphone just because all your friends have it. Find out about services and refuunds.

Buy

How are you going to pay for your phone? Are you going to buy it on the internet or in a store? Take into account the repairs and refunds.

Overview

Do you need a phone - or a new phone- ?

The mobile phone is an essential item nowadays, more people will own cellphones than have running water by 2020. You are out of contact if you dont have a mobile device. However many other gadgets provide the same function and can be used instead of buying a mobile.


Sometimes we buy things because its the trend or the new flashy thing to have, but the newer model of a phone has the same function of the older model - The older is just new and flashy, this means it may have new quirks and better processing power, which can be necessary depending on your plan of use for your phone. Also consider when you crack the screen on your phone. Some people would just go ahead and buy a brand new phone, but new screens can be fixed yourself for as little as $50. If you go to a repair shop it could cost up to $150, but still better than buying a new phone!


Consider your budget.

Mobile phones prices vary from less than $20 to $1300+ and if you are an impulse buyer you may think that generally the most expensive is the best - or the phone that everyone else buys. However there are thousand of different types of phones, all slightly different, with special quirks and better or worse computing power, different operating systems, sound, wifi, screen resolution and more - all these factors should be researched when buying a phone. Your budget will play the biggest role in buying your phone, don't bother looking at phones that are out of your price range. There are many phones that are relatively cheap and still very powerful. Some phones are much cheaper yet also more powerful than the popular phones. This can be due to the companies not advertising and spending more money on improving the phone. Why would you not buy the cheaper and more powerful phone? There are other factors affecting consumer decisions besides price. For example the cheaper phone may have very bad customer care, this would result it in being very difficult to repair or replace your phone. There are many other factors that may affect decisions.


Research!

Consult the internet, its your best friend when it comes to buying phones.


When researching it is easiest to boil down your choice to 3 or 4 different models, then it is easier to find comparisons model to model. Do this by finding out information on the best phones in your price range for your requirements, you may not care about having a fingerprint sensor, so do not buy a phone that has one if it is more expensive. When you have found your found your favourites, read lots of reviews on them and make sure there are no hidden cons like no customer support. Include researching the shops you are considering buying your phone from- online and local - so you know how they handle things like shipping, repairs, refunds and other non product related necessities. Read lots of reviews and don't just read reviews on the same site, reviews can be biased or completely fake.

Consider buying a case and screen protectors, I recommend researching the best cases as well since they can increase the life of your phone greatly.


Buy it!

So you have found the phone you want, and you now you want to buy it. The more convenient option is to just go to your nearest mobile phone shop. Most shops would give you a warranty and if you ever have any problems, you can easily go the shop and complain. The shop would also have to comply with the Australian laws, while online shops outside of Australia do not. That brings us to the other major option; Online shops. Buying online can be much better because you can find the same product much cheaper, but with this convenience comes scams, rip-offs, fakes, and many other problems that are not necessary illegal; they may have problems shipping the phone, you may not be able to get refunds. However these problems do not occur if you take care in shopping safely online. Buy from trusted sites. Shops based in Australia will be much easier to contact. If you pay for a phone using a credit card based service, you can call your bank or that service and usually cancel the payment if you realise it is a scam, do not pay with bitcoin or any other forms of untraceable payments unless you are absolutely certain the seller is real.
